Frequently Asked Questions

Is a metal roof noisier when it rains?

This is a common myth! When installed over solid sheathing and proper insulation, a metal roof is no louder than any other roofing type. In many cases, the attic space and insulation dampen the sound so effectively you won't notice a difference.

How long can I expect a metal roof to last?

One of the biggest draws of metal is its longevity. While traditional asphalt shingles typically last 15 to 20 years, a professionally installed metal roof can easily last 50+ years, your metal roof will probably outlive you.

Can I install a metal roof if I live on the coast?

Absolutely, but material choice is key. If you are within a few miles of the ocean, you should opt for ALUMINUM or COPPER rather than galvanized steel. Aluminum is naturally resistant to salt spray corrosion, ensuring your roof doesn't rust in the salty sea air.

How does the cost compare to traditional shingles?

The upfront cost of metal is generally 2 to 3 times higher than asphalt shingles. However, because you likely won't have to replace it for the rest of your life—and because it can lower cooling costs by reflecting solar heat—it often proves to be the more economical choice over the long term.
